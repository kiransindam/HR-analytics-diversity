Part 04 · Post Strategy

Why This Post Works — The Science Behind It

Every element of this post is engineered with a specific purpose. Here's the breakdown.

🪝
The Hook: "stopped me cold"
LinkedIn hides everything after line 3. The phrase "stopped me cold" creates an emotional gap that makes people click "see more." Curiosity is the algorithm's best friend. Never open with "I'm excited to share."
🔢
Specific Numbers Throughout
2.42 vs 2.41, 70%, 0%, 215, 500 — every claim has a specific number. Vague posts get 3× fewer shares than posts with concrete data. Numbers signal expertise, not opinion.
📖
Story Arc with a Twist
The post builds tension: equal performance → unequal outcomes → zero exec appointments → but the talent exists → young workforce = window is now. This is a classic 5-act structure in 400 words.
💡
The Insight Closer
"This isn't a talent problem — it's a fairness problem." This is a reframe that makes HR professionals and leaders want to share the post. It's not a conclusion, it's a provocation.
🔗
GitHub Link Placed Late
LinkedIn's algorithm penalises external links at the top of posts. The GitHub link is placed after the value, not before. Pro tip: post the link in the first comment instead for even better reach.
💬
Targeted Engagement Ask
"Drop a 💬 if you work in HR, People Analytics, or BI" is specific audience targeting. Specific asks get more comments than generic "thoughts?" The emoji signals warmth, not formality.
#️⃣
10 Precision Hashtags
Mix of high-volume (#DataAnalytics, #PowerBI) and niche (#PeopleAnalytics, #WorkforceAnalytics). 10 hashtags is optimal — more looks like spam, fewer limits discovery. No generic (#life, #growth) tags.
📊
Business Language, Not Tech Language
"Fairness problem" not "bias in the dataset." "Executive pipeline" not "executive-level KPI." Business decision-makers read LinkedIn, not data scientists. Write for the people who hire.
